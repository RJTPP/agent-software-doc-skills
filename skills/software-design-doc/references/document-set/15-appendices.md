## 15. Appendices
- What to include: supporting artifacts that are useful but not central to the main narrative.
- Minimum evidence: appendices referenced from the main SDD when they materially support the design.

### 15.1 Sequence Flows
- What to include: request/response or event-driven flows for key scenarios.
- Minimum evidence: at least one critical interaction sequence is documented.

### 15.2 State Models
- What to include: lifecycle or state-transition definitions for key entities/components.
- Minimum evidence: states and transitions are explicit.

### 15.3 Configuration Matrix
- What to include: configuration surfaces, defaults, and environment-specific differences.
- Minimum evidence: critical runtime configuration is reviewable.

### 15.4 Cache Policy
- What to include: cache keys, TTLs, invalidation strategy, and ownership.
- Minimum evidence: cache behavior is documented for stateful or performance-critical paths.

### 15.5 Security and Privacy Notes
- What to include: trust boundaries, sensitive data handling, and privacy-relevant constraints.
- Minimum evidence: security/privacy implications are visible to reviewers.

### 15.6 Testing Strategy
- What to include: design validation approach across unit, integration, and operational testing.
- Minimum evidence: critical design assumptions are tied to test or verification methods.

### 15.7 Risk Register
- What to include: a more detailed operational or delivery risk table if needed.
- Minimum evidence: major residual risks and owners are explicit.
