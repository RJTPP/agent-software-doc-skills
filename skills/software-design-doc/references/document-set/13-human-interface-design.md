## 13. Human Interface Design
- What to include: user-facing interaction design constraints relevant to implementation.
- Minimum evidence: core flows, key screens, and UX constraints are explicit.

### 13.1 UX Flow Summary
- What to include: primary user journeys and decision points.
- Minimum evidence: at least one critical end-to-end flow is documented.

### 13.2 Key Screens/Wireframes
- What to include: screen inventory, layout intent, and references to mocks if present.
- Minimum evidence: the main interaction surfaces are named and scoped.

### 13.3 Interaction Constraints
- What to include: accessibility, responsiveness, validation, and UX consistency requirements.
- Minimum evidence: constraints are specific enough to guide implementation and QA.
